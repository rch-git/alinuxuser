I got this game through Game Pass for $1, and I finished it fast. I started in late December and rolled credits on Sunday, January 5, 2025, at 9:27 AM. Safe to say I got my dollar’s worth.

This is easily the best Indiana Jones game ever made on PC. I’m genuinely happy it exists. The last Indiana Jones game I played was Emperor’s Tomb. I wanted to like that game, but I really didn’t. The controls were clunky, and it aged badly. I pretty much gave up on the idea of ever getting a truly good Indiana Jones game again. Since Emperor’s Tomb came out in 2003, it took 22 years to get another one.

I’m glad MachineGames made it. I loved Wolfenstein: The New Order, and these guys know how to make a game that feels polished and fun.

## Story

I’ll be honest: over the years, it’s gotten harder for me to stay invested in video game stories. Even when I pay attention, I usually forget most of the plot after I finish.

So with that said, here’s what I remember: Nazis are after Noah’s Ark, and Indiana Jones has to stop them. That’s about all I’ve got. The story takes Indy to a bunch of cool places, and there are definitely some funny moments, but the plot itself felt messy and overcomplicated. It never really clicked for me.

## Presentation

The graphics are excellent. I’m pretty sure this is running on id Tech, and some of the environments look amazing. There are several standout locations. The cliff areas in Sukhothai look great, and the short section in China was especially well done.

The music is also excellent and feels true to the movies.

Special mention goes to the intro. They turned the opening of Raiders of the Lost Ark into a playable level. That alone was awesome. I just wish it lasted longer.

## Gameplay

The gameplay is good, but not top tier.

The weapon system feels strange. Ammo is limited, so you end up relying a lot on hand-to-hand combat and melee weapons. That part can actually be fun when the game stays out of your way.

The biggest gameplay problem is the companion character. She hurts the stealth sections badly. There are moments where she walks right into enemy sightlines, but because enemies can’t really do anything to her, it creates goofy situations where they either shoot at her for no reason or ignore her completely. It looks silly and breaks immersion.

Having an AI sidekick during stealth gameplay is always risky, and this game doesn’t pull it off.

There are also invisible walls, instant fail states, and boss fights that only work one specific way. No room for creativity. You do exactly what the developers want, or you fail. That holds the game back from being truly great.

## Characters and Voice Acting

Most of the supporting cast didn’t do much for me. I skipped a lot of cutscenes because many of the NPCs were forgettable, and some of the voice acting ranged from boring to just okay.

That said, two characters absolutely deliver: Indiana Jones and Emmerich Voss.

Troy Baker does an amazing job as Indy. At times he sounds so close to Harrison Ford that it’s kind of wild. Marios Gavrilis is also excellent as Voss. Whenever those two shared the screen, the game came alive. Those scenes felt like an actual Indiana Jones movie.

## World and Exploration

One thing the game gets right is the sense of adventure. Indy travels all over the world just like in the films.

You visit Connecticut, the Vatican, Giza, the Himalayas, Shanghai, Sukhothai, and the Ziggurat of Ur. The developers did a great job with these locations.

There are also side quests, puzzles, and collectibles everywhere. I mostly ignored them. You can earn money and buy items from shops. I remember needing to buy a camera early on, but after that I barely used the system.

## Final Thoughts

This is a solid action-adventure game and an easy recommendation for Indiana Jones fans.

What I enjoyed most was the combat, the excellent level design, the music, and the scenes between Indy and Voss.

What holds it back are the weak side characters, clumsy stealth mechanics, restrictive boss fights, and some rough gameplay design choices.

I would not recommend paying $69.99 for the standard edition or $99.99 for the premium version. That pricing is crazy for what this game offers.

At around $10? Absolutely worth it.

I’m very glad this game got made. I’d love to see them make a sequel that fixes the weak spots, improves the supporting cast, and tightens up the gameplay. The foundation is here for something even better.

I got Indiana Jones and the Great Circle on Game Pass for just $1. I finished it super fast — started the subscription in late December and beat the game on Sunday, January 5, 2025.

I definitely got more than a dollar’s worth of fun out of it.

This is easily the best Indiana Jones game ever made for PC. I’m really happy it exists. The last one I tried was Indiana Jones and the Emperor’s Tomb back in the day. I really wanted to like it, but the controls were clunky and it just hasn’t aged well. After that, I pretty much gave up on the idea of a good Indy game. It took 22 years for another one to come along.

I’m glad MachineGames made this. I loved Wolfenstein: The New Order, so I knew these guys could deliver. That said, you can kind of feel them playing it safe with the story and characters, like they were apologizing for their art. Might be why some choices feel a little off.

The plot is about Nazis hunting for Noah’s Ark. That’s pretty much all I remember from memory. The story felt kind of convoluted and didn’t always make a ton of sense to me, but it does take Indy to some really cool places.

The visuals are fantastic. This is the id Tech engine (same as Doom) and it looks amazing in a lot of spots. The cliff level in Sukhothai and the short section in China are especially stunning. The music is great too — it really captures the movie feel.

The opening is one of the best parts. They turned the beginning of Raiders of the Lost Ark into a playable level. I wish it was a bit longer, but it was awesome.

Gameplay is solid but not top-tier for me. The weapon system feels weird and ammo is pretty scarce, so you end up doing a lot of hand-to-hand and melee fighting. The companion character really hurts the experience though. She looks kind of silly, and she completely breaks stealth sections. Enemies will shoot at her or just ignore her since she can’t die, which makes those parts feel goofy and poorly done. Not every game can pull off a sidekick like Alyx in Half-Life 2.

The character herself is pretty boring, and a lot of the dialogue is heavy on exposition. Overall, I wasn’t impressed with most of the character interactions. I ended up skipping a bunch of cutscenes because the NPCs and voice acting just weren’t that interesting.

The big exceptions are Indy himself and the villain Emmerich Voss. Troy Baker and Marios Gavrilis both did an incredible job. Troy sounds so much like Harrison Ford that you’d swear it was him. The scenes where Indy and Voss are together feel like watching a movie — those were some of the highlights.

The game sends Indy all over the world like the films: Connecticut, the Vatican, Giza, the Himalayas, Shanghai, Sukhothai, and the Ziggurat of Ur. The levels are really well made.

There are some things that hold it back though. Invisible walls, instant fail states, clunky stealth, and boss fights that only work one specific way (no room for creativity) keep it from being truly great.

This is a solid action-adventure game that every Indiana Jones fan should play. At the full launch price of $70 (or $100 for the premium edition), I wouldn’t recommend it — it doesn’t reach all-time great status. But at around $10? Totally worth it.

There are side quests, puzzles, and collectibles, but I mostly ignored them. You can earn money to buy gear and stuff, but I only did the bare minimum (like buying the camera early on).

What I enjoyed most was the combat when the game didn’t get in its own way, the excellent levels, the Indy/Voss scenes, and the music.

I’m really glad this game got made. I hope they make a sequel that fixes the rough spots — especially the companion. They should make her look and sound better, and keep her out of the actual gameplay, maybe like Farah in Prince of Persia where she’s there but not constantly in your way.

Overall, fun Indy adventure that fans will enjoy, especially if you can get it cheap.
