title: 'RoboCop: Rogue City (PC) Review'
---
When I first heard about RoboCop: Rogue City, I fully expected it to be an awful movie tie-in game. Terrible controls, bad PC port, un-optimized and buggy mess. When the reviews started coming out, I was very surprised to see this game being rated highly. Could this game really be good? Like the Batman games?

I played this via family sharing on Steam. Its a very good game, and I thoroughly recommend this for anyone who likes action games.

There are some absolutely epic moments in the game - killing enemies with the auto 9 with the RoboCop theme playing in the background is an epic feeling. This is pure 80s nostalgia.

Developers did a fantastic job with the visuals. They put Unreal Engine 5 to very good use. Some of the locations look photorealistic. There are many calls backs to the movie. I wouldn't consider myself to be the biggest fan of RoboCop, but if you are, there is plenty here for you.

Music, soundtrack and audio are very good. As I said before, going on a rampage against other robots, with robocop theme playing in the background, with explosions going off everywhere is truly epic.

They created a semi open world here, where you get to walk the streets of old Detroit. Its about as rundown and awful as the real Detroit. Visuals do a good job capturing all this.

One of things I did not like about this game is the busy work. Because the movement is slow (understandable), having to walk (feebly job) back and forth between objectives gets very boring. One of the main reasons I did not bother with side quests is because of the slow movement. It got boring.

Special mention should go to voice acting, especially Peter Weller as RoboCop. This was awesome. I am glad they managed to get him to play RoboCop again. He proved that he only one that can ever play RoboCop.

I did not watch the sequels to the first movie. This game is set after the events of RoboCop 2.

I did not play any attention to the story. I skipped most of the cut scenes, because even though I enjoyed the voice acting of Peter Weller, the story itself was boring to me. I couldn't bother with the internal politics of the police departmnet or care why a corporate overlord hates poor people, and wants to build a utopia. I just wanted to use cool weapons and kill enemies.
