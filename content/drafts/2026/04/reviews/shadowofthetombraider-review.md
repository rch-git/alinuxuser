No one is reading my review years after the game is released to get a buying recommendation. That said, I got this game for free on EGS.

I wish I could comment on the story, but I did not understand it. I did watch some cut scenes in the beginning of the game, but the story did not make a lot of sense. Lara Croft is in Mexico, and she is going to take on the creatively named bad guy organization named Trinity. Why? I do not know. The adventure takes her to some very beautiful and exotic locations in South America. While I did not understand the story, the situations that they put Lara Croft in, are very contrived. Many times, enemies had the option to just kill her, but somehow don't. Simple things that you would expect any competent mercenaries to handle, are fumbled by the enemies. Lara Croft has impenetrable plot armor. She should have died the moment the plane crashed, but whatever. This made me not want to take the story seriously because it was so poorly directed. To the extent I watched the cut scenes, I pretty much zoned out. Voice acting was quite good, and everyone else's was poor. Anytime the little native kid showed up on the screen, I started mashing the space bar. Bad voice acting of all the other characters, poor story in general, plot armor, contrived action sequences, just made me not care about the narrative at all.

The initial Mexico sequence is a bit of slog because you are just walking and talking. It is pretty though.

Music was alright, I suppose. It wasn't anything great like in the Core Design games. It was good, but not anything very memorable.

Visuals is where the game truly shines. This might be the best looking Tomb Raider game ever made. The graphics are fantastic, and the locations in the game are vibrant. I can only imagine if they ever remake Tomb Raider 3, the India levels in this engine will be excellent. The level design is also very good. Combine with the great visuals with top notch level design, and you have an excellent action adventure game. Special mention should be made to the underwater sections. They look incredible.

The biggest complaint I have with this game is something I've had with the other games as well. Why do the RPG elements exist? Why is there a half baked crafting system? Its so boring. This is made by people who do not underannd why fans of Tomb Raider play these games. Its not for the silly crafting and RPG mechanics. Its all filler content, and its poorly implements, and it only exists for the marketing purposes. There is no depth to this, its boring, and its implemented in the most lazy way possible.

I wouldn't mind the goofy weapons systems from the old Core Design games, compared to what we have here.

Also, why are there no dual pistols? Very surprising, and somewhat disappointing.

They tried to make this an open world game, but its all very shallow, and pointless. Instead of focusing on the things that Tomb Raider does well, they waste time and effort adding all these pointless systems into the game. Its just padding. Even more boring are the young Lara sections. Again, should have been a skipable cut scene, but they make you play as the young Lara, and waste my time.

Combat in the game is fine. However, there are situations where the game simple does not let you play it. Case in point, when the jaguars attack. This should have been a fantastic fight with the beasts, but the game gets in the way, and takes control away from you. Also some of the stealth sections are quite goofy.

The visuals, level design, puzzles, and gameplay (RPG bloat nonewithstanding) totally save the game. Just about everything else is a waste of time.

I still recommend playing this game. Its a solid game, and its fun. I think I like this better than Rise of the Tomb Raider. I would have paid $5 for this game. Anything more than that is not worth it in my opinion.

I got all the DLC with my game on EGS, and it was fun to play the game with different costumes.

Its not the gameplay that's the problem. Its all the filler that gets boring. I will never replay this game for that reason - too much filler content that cannot be skipped.

As it stands, it is the best looking Tomb Raider game ever made, and it a lot of fun if you ignore most of the story, and just focus on the gameplay.
