No one is reading my review years after the game is released to get a buying recommendation. That said, I got this game for free on EGS.

I wish I could comment on the story, but I did not understand it. I did watch some cut scenes in the beginning of the game, but the story did not make a lot of sense. Lara Croft is in Mexico, and she is going to take on the creatively named bad guy organization named Trinity. Why? I do not know. The adventure takes her to some very beautiful and exotic locations in South America. While I did not understand the story, the situations that they put Lara Croft in, are very contrived. Many times, enemies had the option to just kill her, but somehow don't. Simple things that you would expect any competent mercenaries to handle, are fumbled by the enemies. Lara Croft has impenetrable plot armor. This made me not want to take the story seriously because it was so poorly directed. To the extent I watched the cut scenes, I pretty much zoned out. Voice acting was not horrible, but it can't make me enjoy bad storytelling.

Music was alright, I suppose. It wasn't anything great like in the Core Design games. It was good, but not anything very memorable.

Visuals is where the game truly shines. This might be the best looking Tomb Raider game ever made. The graphics are fantastic, and the locations in the game are vibrant. I can only imagine if they ever remake Tomb Raider 3, the India levels in this engine will be excellent. The level design is also very good. Combine with the great visuals with top notch level design, and you have an excellent action adventure game. Special mention should be made to the underwater sections. They look incredible. The one

The biggest complaint I have with this game is something I've had with the other games as well. Why do the RPG elements exist? Why is there a half baked crafting system? Its so boring. This is made by people who do not underannd why fans of Tomb Raider play these games. Its not for the silly crafting and RPG mechanics. Its all filler content, and its poorly implements, and it only exists for the marketing purposes. There is no depth to this, its boring, and its implemented in the most lazy way possible.

I wouldn't mind the goofy weapons systems from the old Core Design games, compared to what we have here.

Also, why are there no dual pistols? Very surprising, and somewhat disappointing.
