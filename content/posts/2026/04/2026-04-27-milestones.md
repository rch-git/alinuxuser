##### Financial Detox

For the past few weeks or so, I decided to do a digital detox. I have not followed the news, I did not read any hot takes about the Iran war or about how President Trump should have handled the situation from a podcaster who is completely detached from reality. It added nothing of value to my life, and it occupied precious brain space. This quote from Sherlock Holmes comes to mind -

___“What the deuce is it to me? You say that we go round the sun. If we went round the moon it would not make a pennyworth of difference to me or to my work.”___ - Sherlock Holmes, A Study in Scarlet.

To conserve space in my brain attic, I quit reading all headlines. The middle east could be going to hell in a hand basket, the stock market could be crashing to new lows, and I was none the wiser. It felt good. I also quit looking at the financial markets. I am smart enough to not make any decisions about my portfolio based on headlines, and following a volatile market was not doing me any good. So I quit looking at that too.

On April 16, 2026, as I was wrapping up my trip to Charleston, SC and heading back home, I was stuck in the airport. I was bored, and I decided to check the market. Much to my surprise, S&P 500 was over 7000. The market closes at 7041.28 and this meant my portfolio, which is 100% in US equities has reached a milestone as well.

On November 8, 2016, S&P 500 was trading at `2139.56`, and on November 9, 2016 it closed at `2163.26`. That's over ~1% gain in one day. This was when I started doubting the news. How could they so confidently print someone's opinion as fact? How could they get it so wrong? My trust in the news never recovered from this.

Today, 2460 business days later, the market is now at `7126.03` (as of close on Apr 17, 2026 Friday 08:06:06 PM CDT). During these ~2400 days, not one day has gone by where someone wasn't predicting a stock market collapse, or how President Trump would start World War 3, or a new pandemic that will end all humanity. Not to make light of the bad events that happened, but nothing came anywhere close to what the experts said would happen. Could there be something catastrophic around the corner? Sure. I have no idea, and I don't think anyone does either. That's the point. No one has a clue what is going on, but the talking heads are prepared to pretend like they do. That's the kind of media landscape we are dealing with.

The age old of wisdom of ignoring the headlines, setting sensible goals, and steadily pursuing them, seems to have worked. Of course, my planning, and my actions can only get me so far without the macro economics working in my favor. What good is a wonderful bull market if I panic sold when the market dropped in April 2025? For context, S&P 500 closed at a record high of `6144.15` on February 19, 2025. On April 8, 2025, S&P 500 closed at `4982.77`. This is sometimes referred to as the Libration Day crash.

There was widespread panic. Armchair economists on X were predicting a financial collapse. Everyone was an expert on tariffs. They were arguing the economics of tariffs, when in reality, he was using them to asset economic dominance and get better trade deals. You don't need to be a finance expert to understand this. President Trump was clearly shouting this from the rooftops. It made no difference. The credential class were determined to stay in a state of panic - its like they enjoyed the anxiety. I don't understand this worldview. I quite like the idea that my country is the mightiest country in the world, and is prepared to assert economic dominance in the right situation to make us wealthier. I fully expect people from other countries to dislike the way President Trump went about this, but he was acting in what he believed was best for the country.

When the market hit its lowest point, the panic was in full swing. Trump called them PANICANS. Appropriate. I did nothing different. I did buy the dip a little bit, but I did not have the cashflow, and I had to sit on the sidelines a little bit. At least, I wasn't selling (or making any changes to my asset allocation) like those geniuses on TV and X.

On June 27, 2025, S&P 500 closed at a new all-time high of `6173.07`. This marked full recovery from the tariff scare draw down (and set a new record). The index had already turned positive for the year by May.

Coming to the current situation, we see a similar trend.

| April 15, 2026    | 7,022.95 | New all-time high; S&P 500 surpasses previous record (Jan 28 high) and crosses back above 7,000. Full recovery of all Iran-war losses + new record set.|

All the while, there were people online who were predicting the end of the world. Whether they truly believed these delusions or they were doing this for attention is hard to say. I am not in favor or wars, that said, if a war is inevitable, I'd rather America win by crushing its enemies. It appears that peace has been achieved through strength. As much as I dislike the idea of foreign entanglements, if they are inevitable, lets at least not engage in pointless nation building - and so far, we haven't been doing that, which gives me a good feeling. Can all this change on Monday April 20, 2026? Yes. I know I will not be doing anything different. I will stay the course.

Its rather amusing that the end of the war (as of now) brought with it a collective sigh of relief among those that are not terminally online, and also helped me achieve an important personal financial milestone.

Going forward, I want to think less about finances, and more about life. The last few years have been a relentless pursuit of numbers. Sure it was nice to see the progress, but I must confess, it did not bring me any real happiness. There is no one in my life I can share this with it, who will actually understand the accomplishments (if you want to call it that). I am not someone who is into lifestyle inflation - buying things does not bring me happiness. Quite the opposite, its a cause for stress. I want to pursue real happiness. I wish I knew exactly what that was. For now though, I am glad to to have rediscovered my love for writing.

I am also going to play more video games, and not spend time browsing the internet. I need to figure out a way to do live blogging (writing while I play). I have a couple of ideas.

I want to small the roses, enjoy the gifts I have been given, even if it means I have to travel this path by myself - because there is no one I can share this with. It feels very tacky to brag to friends and family about financial milestones. Inevitably this will bring judgement, and I can certainly do without it.

So what will be different going forward?

Certainly spend more on experiences. Go out more.

For example, today is very uncharacteristic. I do not go out by myself and get a meal. I have done it in the past, but its not very common. However, this is surprisingly nice. I am going to do this more. A few dollars here and there will hardly make any difference in my life at this point.

I get the feeling that being at home has me lazy. I should get out more.

Wins in life are rare. I am going to make it a point to celebrate wins - however inconsequential or unusual as they might be. After all, how many people in the world are celebrating their net worth milestones? I don't think I am the only one, I am sure there are those in the FIRE community doing this very thing every year. I am not in that community, and I do not believe in the concept of FIRE. I do like FI though. Its the RE part that I do not like. We should never celebrate not doing work.

Personally, I Want to do meaningful work for as long as I live.

The relentless pursuit of 4% is not something I want to engage in. Although, arguably, I am very close to that milestone.
